# Sources & Landmark Literature for Shock Management

## Guidelines
* **Surviving Sepsis Campaign Guidelines (2021):** The definitive framework for the management of sepsis and septic shock in the ICU.
    * [Read the SCCM/ESICM Guidelines](https://journals.lww.com/ccmjournal/Fulltext/2021/11000/Surviving_Sepsis_Campaign__International.21.aspx)
* **AHA Scientific Statement on Cardiogenic Shock (2017):** Comprehensive overview of contemporary management of cardiogenic shock.
    * [Read the AHA Statement](https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIR.0000000000000525)

## Foundational Reviews
* **Circulatory Shock (New England Journal of Medicine, 2013):** A core, must-read review on the pathophysiology, diagnosis, and treatment of shock.
    * [Read the NEJM Review](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMra1208943)
* **Vasopressor and Inotrope Use in Shock (New England Journal of Medicine, 2025/Historical):** Foundational concepts for receptor pharmacology and agent selection.
    * [Read the NEJM Review](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMra1507998)

## Diagnostic & POCUS Literature
* **The RUSH Exam (Rapid Ultrasound in Shock):** Original paper detailing the step-by-step approach to ultrasound for undifferentiated hypotension.
    * [Read the RUSH Protocol Paper](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3529329/)
* **Fluid Responsiveness (Intensive Care Medicine):** Assessing dynamic markers over static markers for fluid resuscitation.
    * [Read about Dynamic Fluid Assessment](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24522851/)
