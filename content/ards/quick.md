# ARDS Quick Guide

## 1. Diagnostic Criteria (Berlin Definition)
* **Timing:** Within 1 week of a known clinical insult or new/worsening respiratory symptoms.
* **Imaging:** Bilateral opacities on CXR or CT not fully explained by effusions, lobar/lung collapse, or nodules.
* **Origin of Edema:** Respiratory failure not fully explained by cardiac failure or fluid overload (needs objective assessment like echo if no clear risk factor).
* **Oxygenation (with PEEP or CPAP $\ge$ 5 cmH2O):**
    * **Mild:** PaO2/FiO2 201-300
    * **Moderate:** PaO2/FiO2 101-200
    * **Severe:** PaO2/FiO2 $\le$ 100

## 2. Common Triggers
* **Direct Lung Injury:** Pneumonia (most common), aspiration, pulmonary contusion, inhalation injury.
* **Indirect Lung Injury:** Sepsis (non-pulmonary), severe trauma, massive transfusion (TRALI), pancreatitis.

## 3. Initial Management Protocol
* **Tidal Volume (Vt):** Target 6 mL/kg of Predicted Body Weight (PBW). Range 4-8 mL/kg.
* **Plateau Pressure (Pplat):** Keep $\le$ 30 cmH2O.
* **Oxygenation Target:** PaO2 55-80 mmHg or SpO2 88-95%.
* **PEEP/FiO2 Strategy:** Use ARDSNet low or high PEEP tables depending on severity.
* **Fluids:** Conservative fluid strategy once out of shock (dry lungs = happy lungs).

## 4. Rescue Therapies for Severe ARDS
* **Prone Positioning:** For PaO2/FiO2 < 150. Proning for at least 16 hours/day.
* **Neuromuscular Blockade (Paralytics):** Short course (48 hours) for ventilator dyssynchrony or severe hypoxemia not responding to deep sedation.
* **Inhaled Pulmonary Vasodilators:** iNO or epoprostenol (bridge to other therapies, no proven mortality benefit).
* **ECMO:** V-V ECMO for refractory hypoxemia despite optimization.
