# Sepsis: Quick Reference Guide

## 1. Definition (Sepsis-3)
* **Sepsis:** Life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection. 
* **Organ Dysfunction:** Acute change in total SOFA score $\ge$ 2 points secondary to the infection cause.
* **Septic Shock:** Sepsis with persisting hypotension requiring vasopressors to maintain MAP $\ge$ 65 mm Hg **AND** a serum lactate level > 2 mmol/L (18 mg/dL) despite adequate volume resuscitation.

## 2. The Hour-1 Bundle (Immediate Actions)
*Act immediately upon recognition of sepsis or septic shock.*

1.  **Measure Lactate:** Remeasure if initial lactate is elevated (> 2 mmol/L).
2.  **Obtain Blood Cultures:** Draw *before* administering antibiotics (do not significantly delay abx).
3.  **Administer Broad-Spectrum Antibiotics:** Target suspected source (e.g., Vanco/Zosyn for undifferentiated shock).
4.  **Rapid Fluid Resuscitation:** Administer **30 mL/kg** of crystalloid for hypotension or lactate $\ge$ 4 mmol/L. (Complete within first 3 hours). 
    * *Preferred:* Balanced crystalloids (Lactated Ringer's or Plasmalyte).
5.  **Apply Vasopressors:** Initiate if hypotensive during or after fluid resuscitation to maintain MAP $\ge$ 65 mm Hg.
    * *First-line:* **Norepinephrine**.

## 3. Initial Monitoring
* **Vitals:** Continuous telemetry, frequent BP (consider A-line), O2 saturation.
* **Perfusion:** Capillary refill time, mentation, urine output (> 0.5 mL/kg/hr).
* **Source Control:** Identify and remove/drain the source of infection as rapidly as possible (remove lines, drain abscesses, surgical consult if needed).
