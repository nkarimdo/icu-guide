# SEPSIS — DEEP CLINICAL GUIDE

---

# Pathophysiology

## Core Mechanism
Dysregulated immune response → endothelial injury → microvascular dysfunction → organ failure.

### Key Processes
- Cytokine storm (TNF-α, IL-1, IL-6)
- Capillary leak
- Mitochondrial dysfunction
- Coagulopathy (DIC risk)

---

# Diagnostic Criteria

## Sepsis (Sepsis-3)
Suspected infection + SOFA ≥2

## SOFA Components
- PaO₂/FiO₂
- Platelets
- Bilirubin
- MAP/vasopressors
- GCS
- Creatinine/UOP

---

# Hemodynamic Patterns

### Early (Warm Shock)
- High CO
- Low SVR
- Warm extremities

### Late (Cold Shock)
- Low CO
- Vasoconstriction
- High mortality

---

# Lactate Physiology

Not only hypoperfusion:
- Impaired oxygen utilization
- Adrenergic stimulation
- Mitochondrial failure

Lactate >2 = high mortality risk.

---

# Initial Management

## Fluids

Preferred:
- Balanced crystalloids

Avoid:
- Excess saline → hyperchloremic acidosis

Fluid responsiveness tests:
- Passive leg raise
- Stroke volume variation

---

## Vasopressor Strategy

### First Line
Norepinephrine

Mechanism:
- α1 vasoconstriction
- Minimal tachycardia

### Add-On Agents
Vasopressin:
- Non-catecholamine pathway

Epinephrine:
- For refractory shock

Phenylephrine:
- Rare use (tachyarrhythmias)

---

## Steroids

Indication:
- Refractory septic shock

Regimen:
- Hydrocortisone 200 mg/day

Mechanism:
- Restores vascular responsiveness

---

# Antibiotic Strategy

## Timing
Within 1 hour → mortality reduction.

## Principles
- Broad coverage initially
- De-escalate after cultures

## Typical Regimens

### Unknown Source
- Vanc + Cefepime or Zosyn

### Pneumonia
- Vanc + Cefepime + Azithro

### Intra-abdominal
- Zosyn or Carbapenem

---

# Organ Support

## Renal
CRRT indications:
- Refractory acidosis
- Hyperkalemia
- Volume overload

## Respiratory
ARDS protocol:
- Low tidal volume ventilation
- Proning

---

# Source Control

Time-critical:
Delay >12 hours significantly increases mortality.

Examples:
- Drain abscess
- Remove infected hardware
- Surgical washout

---

# Prognostic Indicators

High mortality:
- Lactate >4
- Persistent hypotension
- Multi-organ failure
- Immunocompromise

---

# Advanced Topics

## Sepsis-Induced Cardiomyopathy
- Reversible LV dysfunction
- Low EF
- Elevated troponin

## Immunoparalysis Phase
- Increased secondary infections
- Lymphocyte apoptosis

---

# Common Errors

- Giving pressors before adequate fluids
- Narrow antibiotics too early
- Ignoring lactate trends
- Delayed source control
