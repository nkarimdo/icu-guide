# Quick Guide: ICU Ventilator Management

## 1. Initial Setup (The "Safe" Start)
When intubating a standard patient (non-ARDS, non-obstructive), use these baseline settings:
* **Mode:** Assist-Control Volume Control (AC-VC)
* **Tidal Volume (Vt):** 6–8 mL/kg of **Ideal Body Weight (IBW)** (Not actual weight!)
* **Respiratory Rate (RR):** 14–18 breaths/min
* **FiO2:** 100% initially (rapidly titrate down to maintain SpO2 92-96%)
* **PEEP:** 5 cmH2O

## 2. Adjusting Based on ABG
* **High PaCO2 (Acidosis):** Increase minute ventilation ($V_E = RR \times Vt$). Increase RR first. If RR is maxed out without causing auto-PEEP, increase Vt cautiously.
* **Low PaCO2 (Alkalosis):** Decrease minute ventilation. Decrease RR.
* **Low PaO2 (Hypoxia):** Increase FiO2 or increase PEEP.
* **High PaO2 (Hyperoxia):** Decrease FiO2 (goal < 50% to avoid oxygen toxicity), then decrease PEEP.

## 3. High Pressure Alarm Troubleshooting
Perform an inspiratory hold to check the **Plateau Pressure ($P_{plat}$)**.
* **High Peak Pressure, Normal Plateau ($<30$ cmH2O):** This is a **resistance** problem.
    * *Causes:* Biting the tube, mucus plug, bronchospasm, kinked circuit.
    * *Action:* Suction, give bronchodilators, check the tube, consider sedation.
* **High Peak Pressure, High Plateau ($>30$ cmH2O):** This is a **compliance** problem.
    * *Causes:* ARDS, pneumothorax, pulmonary edema, pneumonia, right mainstem intubation, abdominal compartment syndrome.
    * *Action:* Chest X-ray, ultrasound, diuresis, adjust Vt, check tube depth.

## 4. The Crashing Ventilated Patient (DOPE Mnemonic)
Disconnect from the vent and manually bag the patient with 100% O2. If they improve, it's a vent issue. If not, consider:
* **D**isplacement (Tube pulled out or right mainstem)
* **O**bstruction (Mucus plug, biting)
* **P**neumothorax (Tension - needs immediate decompression)
* **E**quipment failure (Ventilator malfunction, disconnected tubing)
