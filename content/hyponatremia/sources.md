# Hyponatremia: Key Literature & Sources

The management algorithms and limits outlined in the quick and deep guides are heavily drawn from the following landmark consensus guidelines and reviews.

1. **European Clinical Practice Guideline on Hyponatremia (2014)**
   * *Overview:* The most commonly referenced European framework, leaning heavily toward symptom-guided treatment and strict limits to avoid ODS.
   * *Link:* [European Journal of Endocrinology - Hyponatremia Guidelines](https://eje.bioscientifica.com/view/journals/eje/170/3/G1.xml)

2. **US Expert Panel Recommendations (2013)**
   * *Overview:* Provides the framework for the "DDAVP clamp" and highly practical guidance on managing severe hyponatremia and overcorrection.
   * *Link:* [The American Journal of Medicine - Expert Panel](https://www.amjmed.com/article/S0002-9343(13)00728-5/fulltext)

3. **Adrogué and Madias: Hyponatremia (NEJM, 2000)**
   * *Overview:* The classic two-part review that introduced the widespread use of the Adrogué-Madias formula for predicting serum sodium changes.
   * *Link:* [NEJM - Hyponatremia Part 1](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJM200005253422107)

4. **UpToDate: Overview of the treatment of hyponatremia in adults**
   * *Overview:* A continuously updated synthesis of literature, providing excellent real-world flowcharts for floor management and correction rates.
   * *Link:* [UpToDate - Hyponatremia Treatment](https://www.uptodate.com/contents/overview-of-the-treatment-of-hyponatremia-in-adults) (Requires subscription access).
