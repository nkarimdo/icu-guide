# Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) - Quick Reference

## 1. KDIGO Definition
AKI is defined by any of the following:
* Increase in serum creatinine (SCr) by $\ge$ **0.3 mg/dL** within 48 hours.
* Increase in SCr to $\ge$ **1.5 times baseline**, known or presumed to have occurred within the prior 7 days.
* Urine volume $<$ **0.5 mL/kg/h** for 6 hours.

## 2. KDIGO Staging
| Stage | Serum Creatinine (SCr) | Urine Output (UO) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | 1.5 - 1.9x baseline **OR** $\ge$ 0.3 mg/dL increase | < 0.5 mL/kg/h for 6-12 hours |
| **2** | 2.0 - 2.9x baseline | < 0.5 mL/kg/h for $\ge$ 12 hours |
| **3** | 3.0x baseline **OR** increase to $\ge$ 4.0 mg/dL **OR** initiation of RRT | < 0.3 mL/kg/h for $\ge$ 24 hours **OR** Anuria for $\ge$ 12 hours |

## 3. The 3-Bucket Framework
1.  **Pre-Renal (Perfusion):** Hypovolemia (bleed, GI loss), reduced effective circulating volume (CHF, cirrhosis), hemodynamics (NSAIDs, ACEi/ARBs, sepsis).
2.  **Intrinsic (Parenchyma):**
    * **Tubular:** ATN (ischemia, contrast, toxins).
    * **Interstitial:** AIN (drugs like PPIs/NSAIDs/antibiotics, infections).
    * **Glomerular:** Glomerulonephritis (IgA, Lupus, ANCA).
    * **Vascular:** Atheroembolic, TMA, HUS/TTP.
3.  **Post-Renal (Plumbing):** BPH, nephrolithiasis, malignancy, kinked Foley. *Always check a bladder scan/PVR early.*

## 4. Emergency Dialysis Indications (AEIOU)
* **A**cidosis: Refractory metabolic acidosis.
* **E**lectrolytes: Refractory hyperkalemia.
* **I**ngestions: Toxic alcohols, salicylates, lithium.
* **O**verload: Refractory volume overload (e.g., pulmonary edema).
* **U**remia: Uremic encephalopathy, pericarditis, bleeding.
